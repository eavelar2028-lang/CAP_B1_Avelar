🦇 MY FUTURE 🦇

While exploring the basics of code, I learned the importance of organization and progress. When coding a new project, organization was critical in order to efficiently prevent errors within my own code. I am able to apply these same organization tactics to my own progress in other classes by managing my time and neatly keeping track of my to do lists. Similar to the problem solving process in a math class, coding requires everything to be set and in an orderly fashion. My Unit 3 Project displays the organization needed to keep a game cautiously organized and functioning. With one line of code out of order, my project would run the risk of not working as intended. Taking the same precautions in math, I prioritize order and accuracy when writing out my work. Even though I don't envision myself pursuing computer science related issues in the future, my experience coding helped me obtain a better understanding of myself and javascript. While taking computer science, I learned more about the studying habits that best help me get work done. I noticed I work best independently and have a quicker understanding of topics when they are visually described to me. An area of weakness I struggle with is memory. Having to constantly refer back to past assignments, I don't always remember the specific tags and purpose of every code. Constant repetition allows for me to continuously grow in understanding and eventually memory.
